💡How the Plan view works

A plan is one month of your budget. You organise money into groups (categories like Housing, Giving, Groceries), and each group contains items (individual line entries).

  • FIXED groups are costs you've committed to — bills, subscriptions, long-term savings.
  • VARIABLE groups are flexible living costs — credit or debit card payoffs, personal allowances, shopping budgets.
  • 🤲 Giving toggle on a group flags it as giving — shown separately on Home.
  • 💰 Saving toggle on an item links it to a goal and counts toward your progress.
  • Paid checkbox marks whether each bill or contribution has actually been paid. Only ticked items count toward goal progress.

💡Charts show historical totals from saved plans, month by month. This is for looking back — not real-time spending.

💡How goals work in Yesanio

Goals are long-term targets you're saving toward — an emergency fund, a holiday, a car. In Yesanio, goals are not edited directly. Instead:

  1. Plan an item toward your goal (e.g. "Holiday Fund" or "Emergency Fund" in any FIXED group).
  2. Click the 💰 toggle to mark it as a saving contribution.
  3. Save the plan. A matching goal is created automatically.
  4. Set a target here. The goal's balance grows as you tick the Paid box on each month's contribution.

The date window lets you count contributions only from a specific time range — useful for yearly goals like "Holiday 2026". Leave both dates blank to count forever.

If you see "+£X pending", it means items are flagged as savings but their Paid box isn't ticked yet. Tick Paid once the contribution actually happens, and it counts toward progress.

About Yesanio

Yesanio is a self-hosted household budgeting tool built around the "pay yourself first" principle. Every pound gets a job before the month begins — savings and giving are set aside first, not last.
